Pharmacy Technology

Certified Pharmacy Technicians play an integral role in supporting Pharmacists in all settings, including community pharmacy, hospitals and health-systems, and long-term care pharmacy. Today's medication-use systems rely heavily on pharmacy technicians. Pharmacy Technician students must complete 180 externship hours, complete a formal training program or on-the-job training and pass a national exam in order to become licensed with the Utah Division of Occupational and Professional Licensing, State Board of Pharmacy.

Students will gain knowledge in the following:

  • Drug Nomenclature
  • Maintain patient profile, manage inventory, and strengthen customer service skills
  • Receive written prescription or refill requests and verify that information is complete and accurate
  • Pre-pack bulk medicines, fill bottles with prescribed medications, and type and affix labels
  • Pharmacy Law
  • Understand the various routes of administration and the factors that influence them
  • Gain strong pharmaceutical mathematical skills and business math
  • Sterile and Non-sterile medication compounding
